Oracle Red Bull Racing enters the 2026 Formula 1 season with bold changes under a major new regulatory era. 
For the first time, the team will run its own power unit developed with Ford, leaving Honda after years of collaboration.
They unveiled a striking glossy livery on their RB22 car ahead of the season. Max Verstappen stays as lead driver alongside 
rising star Isack Hadjar, promoted from Red Bull’s junior outfit. Team Principal Laurent Mekies leads Red Bull’s bid to return to 
the front as F1’s technical landscape evolves.

DRIVERS

Max Verstappen

Max Verstappen is a Dutch Formula 1 driver widely regarded as one of the most talented racers of his generation. 
Born on September 30, 1997, he is the son of former F1 driver Jos Verstappen. Max made his Formula 1 debut in 2015 at just 17 years 
old, becoming the youngest driver to start a Grand Prix. In 2016, he became the youngest race winner in F1 history after winning on 
his Red Bull debut in Spain. Verstappen won consecutive World Championships in 2021, 2022, and 2023, earning recognition for his speed,
confidence, and relentless racing style.

Isac Hadjar

Isack Hadjar is a French-Algerian racing driver and a member of the Red Bull Junior Team. Born on September 28, 2004,
in Paris, France, he began karting at a young age before progressing through the junior single-seater categories. Hadjar gained attention
with strong performances in Formula 3 and later in Formula 2, where he showed impressive speed and racecraft. Known for his determination 
and aggressive driving style, he quickly became one of Red Bull’s most promising young talents. His development within the Red Bull program
has positioned him as a potential future Formula 1 driver.
